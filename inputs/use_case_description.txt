The system under study is a web-based educational platform designed to support remote teaching and learning in a 
realistic and structured environment. The platform facilitates interaction between two primary user roles, 
teachers and students, enabling the creation, management, and participation in online courses and synchronous 
virtual classes. Its functionality covers course administration, real-time communication, user management, 
scheduling, and content sharing, aiming to replicate a complete classroom experience in a virtual setting.

Teachers are responsible for creating and managing courses by providing essential information such as course 
titles and optional multimedia descriptions, including formatted text, images, and external links. Each course 
may contain multiple classes, which can be scheduled by specifying attributes such as title, description, date, 
time, and enrolled students. Teachers can also upload learning materials associated with each class and 
optionally enable discussion forums to support asynchronous interaction. The system allows full flexibility, 
enabling teachers to update or modify courses and class information at any time.

A key feature of the platform is the support for synchronous virtual classrooms. Teachers can initiate live 
sessions where they control the flow of the class, manage audio and video streams, monitor participants, and 
regulate speaking permissions. A structured “intervention turn” mechanism allows students to request speaking 
rights, which can be granted or denied by the teacher, ensuring organized communication during live sessions. 
Students can interact through chat, participate in discussions, and contribute using audio or video when 
authorized.

Students primarily interact with the platform through personalized dashboards that list their enrolled courses 
and scheduled activities. Within each course, they can access learning materials, view upcoming classes, and 
participate in forum discussions if enabled by the teacher. During live sessions, students can attend virtual 
classes, view real-time audio and video streams, and request speaking opportunities. They also have access to 
relevant class metadata, including schedules, duration, and participant lists.

The system includes standard authentication and profile management functionalities. Users must register using a 
valid email address and complete CAPTCHA verification during registration. After authentication, users can 
log in and manage personal information such as usernames, passwords, and profile pictures. Additionally, a 
calendar feature is provided to help users visualize and manage all scheduled classes and activities.

Overall, the platform provides a comprehensive simulation of traditional classroom dynamics, supporting both 
synchronous and asynchronous modes of instruction. Its design emphasizes usability, structured interaction, 
and fine-grained control over teaching activities, making it suitable for realistic educational scenarios 
involving both teachers and students.
